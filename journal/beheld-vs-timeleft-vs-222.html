BeHeld vs. TimeLeft vs. 222: which is built for people who come back?

An honest look at three ways to meet people in NYC — what each one is genuinely good at, and the one thing that separates a great first night from a friendship.

Written by the BeHeld team · Updated July 2026

TimeLeft and 222 are very good at one thing: the first night. A table of strangers, a spark, a story to tell on Monday. That's not a small thing — in a city this size, getting six interesting people into one room on a Wednesday is real work, and they do it well.

What neither is built to do is give you the second night with the same people — the one where a stranger quietly becomes someone who knows your coffee order. That's the whole difference between the three of us, so let's be honest about all of it: how each one works, what it costs, who it's for, and where each is genuinely the right call.

A note on fairness: we run one of the three services compared here, so we've tried to be scrupulous. Everything below reflects each service as of mid-2026, drawn from their own public materials. These companies iterate fast — for exact current pricing and cities, check timeleft.com and 222.place directly.

The honest version of what each one is

TimeLeft — dinner with five strangers, every Wednesday

TimeLeft is the biggest name in "dinner with strangers," running weekly dinners in 200+ cities. You answer a short personality questionnaire, and its algorithm seats you with five people it thinks you'll click with. The restaurant is revealed a day or two before; tables often use conversation-prompt cards, and some groups carry on to an optional drink nearby afterward. It runs on a small monthly subscription, and you pay for your own meal like any night out. It's spontaneous, low-commitment, and genuinely easy to try.

222 — AI-matched hangouts that "engineer chance"

222 is an app that uses matching to curate small groups for real-life experiences — dinners, comedy nights, and other outings — across cities including New York and LA. Its pitch is "engineering chance": using data to recreate the kind of serendipitous meeting a big city usually leaves to luck. It leans younger and more app-native than a standing dinner series, and it's built around discovering your city as much as meeting a fixed group.

BeHeld — the same room, the same faces, on purpose

BeHeld is built for adults in their 30s and 40s who want connection that continues. You apply (a short application, not a swipe), we build a small room around what you actually have in common, and instead of dinner-table small talk you do hands-on activities that reveal character. The part that makes us different comes after: you tell us who you'd want to see again, and if it's mutual, we put you back in the same room with them. We don't sort it into "friends" or "dating" — you meet people who fit and let the relationship find its own shape.

Where TimeLeft is genuinely the right call

If you want the lowest-friction way to have dinner with new people this week — no application, no waiting, just show up Wednesday — TimeLeft is hard to beat. It's perfect when you're new to a city and want volume: lots of first meetings, fast, to widen the top of the funnel. If your goal is "get out of the house and talk to strangers more often," start there.

Where 222 is genuinely the right call

If you're in your twenties, live on your phone, and want to discover your city as much as meet people — a comedy night here, a dinner there, always something new — 222's on-demand, algorithm-driven variety fits that rhythm. It's built for breadth and spontaneity, for people who like the surprise of not knowing who or what they'll get.

Where BeHeld is the right call

Here's the pattern we kept hearing from people who'd tried the others: "The dinner was lovely. I never saw any of them again." That's not a flaw in TimeLeft or 222 — it's the design. The one-off model optimises for the first meeting, and the first meeting is the easy part. The hard part, the part that actually cures the specific loneliness of being surrounded but unknown, is the fifth time you're in a room with someone.

BeHeld is for the person who doesn't want more first dates with friendship — who's tired of introducing themselves and wants to be recognised when they walk in. We keep the rooms small, we build them around common ground, and we bring the same faces back. It asks more of you (an application, a little commitment to showing up) because closeness does. If that trade sounds right, that's us.

The one-line version

TimeLeft and 222 are the best way to meet a lot of people once. BeHeld is the best way to meet a few people again — until "strangers" quietly becomes "your people."

Frequently asked

Is BeHeld more expensive than TimeLeft or 222?

They price differently. TimeLeft runs on a small monthly subscription plus the cost of your own meal; 222 prices per experience through its app. BeHeld is $65 for a single event, which covers your seat and the matching, with recurring membership opening in October 2026. We're not the cheapest way to have dinner once — we're built for the value that shows up over repeated evenings, not a single seat.

Is BeHeld for dating or for friendship?

Neither, exactly — and that's on purpose. Some people find friends, some find a partner, some find community. We build rooms around genuine compatibility and let the relationship become whatever it's meant to be, rather than forcing it into a category on night one.

Can I just try one event before committing?

Yes. The best way to know if BeHeld is for you is to be in the room once — single events are $65, no membership required. See what's coming up.

What makes the matching different from an algorithm?

We read every application ourselves — no bots, no swipe. Then we go looking for a room where you'd actually fit, and after the event you tell us who you'd like to see again so future rooms get sharper. It's less "here are five matches" and more "here are people we think you'll want to keep knowing."
