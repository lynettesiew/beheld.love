Good questions

BeHeld FAQ.

How is BeHeld different from dinner and social networking events?

Most events help you meet people. BeHeld helps meaningful connections grow. We swap forced small talk for hands-on activities, then bring you back together with the people you want to see again. We never define what a connection should be - we let it become what it becomes, however it's meant to be.

Who is BeHeld for?

BeHeld is for people who want to build meaningful relationships, not just meet more people. Our members show up with intention, invest in the connections they make, and understand that to have a village, you have to be a villager first.

Who will be in the room with me?

We thoughtfully curate each group based on life stage and values. We look for enough common ground to make connection easier, without filling the room with people exactly like you. It starts with your application.

What kind of activities can I expect?

Activities evolve from event to event, but the blueprint stays the same: make meeting people easier, help you feel seen, and make reconnecting seamless.


You'll start with a playful icebreaker, followed by a group activity built for laughter and shared moments. If someone stands out, you can leave them a voice note which let us know you'd like to see them again.
The evening ends with deeper questions and a "Made for You" activity where you create something for another person based on what you noticed about them.
How does BeHeld help connections continue?

Meaningful relationships take effort, but knowing what to do nextshouldn't. Each week you get a menu of side quests, from low effort to high: show up, leave a voice note, check in on someone, host a walk. You decide how you want to show up.Showing up earns points that unlock more matches and deeper quests. Miss something you committed to and you lose a little - then win it back by showing up again.

Because having a village starts with being a villager.

Where and when are BeHeld events held?

Our events runs every Thursday at different locations in New York City. Join the waitlist and we'll invite you when your room is ready — upcoming dates live on the Events page.

How do you keep the room safe?

Every participant agrees to The BeHeld Code — a short agreement about how we treat each other, with serious consequences for anyone whose behavior threatens the trust of the room.
